# Random Poster Design: Polar Night Mountain Lake

## Goal

Create a single vertically oriented poster image with a cold night landscape mood. The result should feel finished and display-ready rather than like a rough concept sketch. The image is intentionally simple: atmospheric, quiet, and visually cohesive.

## Visual Direction

The poster uses a `polar night mountain lake` concept. The composition should center on a calm nocturnal landscape with layered mountain silhouettes, a cold moon, thin mist, and a reflective lake surface. The visual priority is serenity and depth, not realism or dense detail.

The piece should read as a design-forward landscape poster rather than a literal photograph. Shapes can be slightly stylized and simplified as long as the image still clearly communicates sky, mountains, water, and reflected light.

## Composition

The canvas is portrait-oriented. The top section is a dark sky with a smooth gradient from deep blue into near-black. A sparse field of stars can be included, but only as light texture. The middle section holds the moon and receding mountain layers. The bottom section contains the lake and mirrored reflections.

Depth should come from stacked silhouettes and restrained contrast. Foreground mountains should be slightly darker and more defined than distant layers. The lake reflection should echo the moon and mountain shapes without becoming overly sharp.

## Color and Light

The palette is cold and limited. Primary tones are deep navy, indigo, slate blue, and cool white. Avoid warm sunset hues, neon accents, or saturated multicolor treatments.

Light should be concentrated around the moon and its reflection. Mist can soften transitions between mountain layers and help the scene feel still and expansive. Highlights should stay controlled so the poster keeps a quiet, nocturnal mood.

## Rendering Approach

The implementation should generate a local image file rather than depend on an external image generation API. A procedural or vector-based approach is acceptable if it can produce a polished final image. Smooth gradients, soft blur-like transitions, and clean edge control matter more than complexity.

The artifact should be suitable as a wallpaper-like poster. No large text is required. If any text is added at all, it should remain minimal and secondary to the image.

## Quality Bar

The output should look intentional and complete: balanced spacing, no visual clutter, no accidental overlaps, and no off-canvas elements. The final result should preserve a calm atmosphere and avoid turning into a generic fantasy illustration or a busy sci-fi scene.

## Deliverable

Produce one portrait poster image in the workspace. A PNG is preferred for the final exported artifact.
