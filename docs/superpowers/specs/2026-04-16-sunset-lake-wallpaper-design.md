# Sunset Lake Wallpaper Design

## Goal

Create one portrait-oriented image for phone wallpaper use. The result should read immediately as a sunset-lit lake landscape, feel calm and high-end, and stay clean enough to work behind app icons and lock-screen UI.

## Approved Direction

The approved concept is `natural landscape + phone wallpaper + sunset golden light + lakeside afterglow`.

This is not intended to be a literal photo reproduction. The image should sit between illustration and art poster: clearly recognizable as a real landscape, but simplified and composed with deliberate graphic control.

## Composition

The canvas should use a vertical phone-wallpaper aspect ratio, targeting `1440 x 3120` or the same `19.5:9` proportion. The upper portion should reserve space for the glowing sunset sky and a few layered clouds catching warm edge light. The middle portion should establish depth with distant mountain silhouettes. The lower portion should hold a calm lake surface with a controlled reflected light path.

The composition should avoid clutter. The focal read is a band of warm sunset light crossing the sky and continuing into the lake reflection. Large shapes should stay clean so the wallpaper remains legible when icons sit over the image.

## Visual Language

The overall mood should be quiet, restrained, and polished rather than hyper-detailed. The image should feel more like a carefully art-directed landscape poster than a busy AI-generated scene.

Edges and layers should be intentional. Mountains can be simplified into strong silhouettes with subtle tonal separation. Clouds should be sparse and placed to support the light source rather than dominate the frame. The lake reflection should be soft and believable without becoming mirror-sharp.

## Color and Light

The palette should center on `amber gold`, `soft orange-red sunset glow`, and `deep teal-blue mountain shadow`. Saturation should stay controlled. Avoid neon color, muddy brown, or overly dramatic rainbow skies.

The warmest values should be concentrated around the sunset zone and its reflected path in the water. Darker cool tones should anchor the mountains and the lower edge of the frame so the image keeps depth and contrast without feeling harsh.

## Wallpaper Constraints

Because the image is meant for a phone wallpaper, the design should preserve visual breathing room. The top and central areas must remain clean enough that common lock-screen overlays and app icons do not destroy the composition.

The wallpaper should still look balanced under light cropping on common phone screens. Important visual elements should stay away from the extreme edges.

## Rendering Approach

Generate a local final image file in the workspace. A programmatic, vector-like, or painted digital approach is acceptable as long as the output looks finished. Prioritize controlled gradients, clean layering, and coherent lighting over raw complexity.

Do not add large typography. If any text is present, it should be minimal enough that the image still functions primarily as a wallpaper, but the preferred outcome is image-only.

## Quality Bar

The finished piece should satisfy all of the following:

1. It reads instantly as a sunset lake landscape.
2. It remains clean and usable as a phone wallpaper.
3. It feels calm, premium, and durable rather than flashy or overworked.

The final composition should avoid accidental overlaps, noisy texture, excessive small detail, or generic fantasy styling.

## Deliverable

Produce one high-resolution portrait image in the workspace. `PNG` is the preferred final format.
